📅 Key Timeline

Major milestones to track through the voluntary retirement process

Now — Before Offer Details
Gather documents, understand your benefits, prepare questions for your financial planner. Get organized now so you can make an informed decision quickly.
May 7, 2026 (Expected) Offer Details Released
Microsoft releases the voluntary retirement program details. Review severance package, timeline, benefits continuation, stock provisions, and any special terms.
After Offer — Review Period
Meet with financial planner, consult an employment attorney if needed, discuss with family. Don't rush the decision.
Decision Deadline (TBD)
Deadline to accept or decline. Typically 21-45 days to decide + 7 days revocation after signing (OWBPA for employees 40+). Exact date will be in the offer.
If Accepting — Transition Period
Execute your personal checklist: roll over accounts, transition insurance, secure personal files, notify contacts, use remaining benefits.
Last Day at Microsoft
Final access to systems, email, and benefits. Ensure everything is transitioned before this date. Back up personal data.
Post-Departure — First 90 Days
Activate new insurance within qualifying event windows. Complete account rollovers. Decompress. Start your next chapter.

✅ Pre-Departure Checklist

Everything to take care of before your last day. Export to Excel to track your own progress.

HR & Administrative

Review the full voluntary retirement offer in detail Severance amount, payment schedule, signing bonus/clawback provisions, non-compete clauses, release of claims
Understand the decision deadline and revocation period Typically 21-45 days to decide + 7-day revocation after signing (OWBPA if age 40+)
Consider having an employment attorney review the offer Especially important for long-tenured employees — ensure terms are fair and understand what you're signing
Confirm your official last day and what it means for benefits end dates
Be prepared: if going to a competitor, you may have just a few days to wrap up Even with 2 weeks’ notice, MS may walk you out quickly if joining a competitor. Get personal items in order early.
Understand Discretionary Time Off (DTO) implications Microsoft uses DTO (not accrued PTO). Verify if there are any payout provisions in the retirement offer or if DTO has no cash value at departure.
Request your full HR file / employment records Performance reviews, tenure confirmation letter, compensation history
Get letters of recommendation / references while still employed

Financial Accounts & Equity

Review all unvested RSUs — what vests before last day, what's forfeited Check your next vesting dates against your potential last day. Negotiate timing if possible.
ESPP — understand current enrollment period When does the current purchase period end? Will you still be employed? What happens to contributions if you leave mid-period?
401(k) — plan your rollover strategy Roll to IRA? Leave in Fidelity? Mega Backdoor Roth considerations. Do NOT cash out — tax penalties are severe.
Confirm you are 100% vested in 401(k) employer match If you've been at MS for 3+ years, you should be fully vested. Verify on Fidelity.
Review outstanding stock sales / tax lots for capital gains planning
Download all Fidelity / MS stock plan statements and transaction history You may lose access to historical data after departure
Review HSA balance and investment allocation HSA is yours forever — triple tax advantaged. Keep receipts for future reimbursement.
Check if you have a Deferred Compensation Plan — understand distribution rules
401(k) IRS contribution limit — important if joining a new employer this year The annual 401(k) limit does NOT reset when you change jobs. If you maxed out at MS, you can’t contribute at your new employer until next year. Do the math to avoid over-contributing.
If your next employer doesn’t match 401(k), consider maxing out your MS 401(k) before leaving
Check stock grant vesting dates — align departure timing with next vest if possible

Insurance & Benefits Transition

Evaluate health insurance options for yourself and dependents See the Benefits & Insurance tab for a full comparison of options
Review MS life insurance — convert or get a private policy BEFORE leaving MS group life is typically 1-2x salary. You can usually convert to an individual policy within 31 days of leaving. Get quotes NOW while employed.
Disability insurance — evaluate if you need personal coverage going forward
Schedule pending dental, vision, and medical appointments before benefits end Get checkups, fill prescriptions, order contacts/glasses, complete any pending procedures
FSA — spend remaining balance before departure (use it or lose it) FSA funds typically must be used by your last day or within a short grace period
Maximize HSA contributions before last day (front-load if possible)
Use Perks+ benefits before leaving (aka.ms/perksplus) Wellness reimbursement, fitness, ergonomic equipment — use it or lose it
Get new glasses / contacts using vision benefits before coverage ends
Use remaining wellness benefits (gym reimbursement, mental health, etc.)
Take advantage of donation matching while still eligible (aka.ms/give)
Get bicycle commuter reimbursement if applicable

Professional & Relationships

Personally reach out to key colleagues and mentors Exchange personal contact info. Your professional network is your most valuable career asset.
Check non-compete / non-solicitation clauses in your agreement Important for consulting or joining competitors — know what's restricted and for how long
Understand IP assignment clauses — what you can take vs. what stays at MS
Update LinkedIn profile (don't announce departure prematurely)

💰 Financial Planner Meeting Prep

Documents to gather and key questions to ask — whether using a financial planner or doing it yourself

🎯 Key Goals for Your Financial Review 1) Evaluate the retirement offer terms objectively
2) Assess overall financial health with MS income stopping
3) Create a transition financial plan with runway estimates
4) Optimize tax strategy for your departure year

Documents to Gather

The voluntary retirement offer letter / package details
Current total compensation statement from MS Base salary, bonus targets, RSU grant schedule, ESPP details
Most recent RSU / stock vesting schedule What's unvested and will be forfeited vs. what vests before last day
401(k) statement from Fidelity (including Mega Backdoor Roth if applicable)
All brokerage / investment account statements
HSA account statement and balance
Current ESPP enrollment details and contribution rate
Last 2 years of tax returns
Social Security statement (ssa.gov)
Monthly household budget / expense breakdown
Spouse's / partner's compensation and benefits summary (if applicable) Important for modeling household income and insurance options
Outstanding debts — mortgage balance, car loans, etc.
All life insurance policies (MS group + personal)

Questions to Discuss

🔎 Evaluating the Offer

  • Is the severance amount reasonable for my tenure?
  • How will severance payments be taxed? Lump sum vs. installments — which is better?
  • What's the total value of unvested RSUs I'm forfeiting?
  • Does the offer include any accelerated vesting provisions?
  • Are there any clawback or repayment provisions I should know about?

💲 Cash Flow & Runway

  • How many months/years of runway do I have without MS income?
  • What's our monthly burn rate vs. available income (spouse, severance, etc.)?
  • Should I build a larger cash reserve before leaving?
  • At what point would I need to generate income?

📈 Investment & Tax Strategy

  • Should I sell MSFT shares for diversification before or after leaving?
  • Tax-loss harvesting opportunities in the departure year?
  • Roth conversion strategy — lower income year = opportunity?
  • 401(k) rollover timing — before or after leaving? Direct rollover to Roth?
  • Mega Backdoor Roth — any final contributions to maximize?
  • Capital gains management given potentially reduced income year

🏥 Insurance

  • COBRA vs. spouse's plan vs. ACA marketplace — cost comparison?
  • Life insurance — convert MS group policy or get new term life?
  • Do I need umbrella / personal liability insurance changes?
  • Long-term disability — do I need private coverage?

💼 If Starting a Business or Consulting

  • Best business structure (LLC, S-Corp) for tax efficiency?
  • Tax advantages of self-employment (Solo 401k, SEP IRA, health insurance deduction)?
  • How much startup capital should I earmark?
  • When should I establish the entity — before or after leaving MS?

🔮 Long-term Planning

  • How does this change my retirement timeline / projections?
  • Social Security optimization — projected benefits at 62/67/70?
  • Estate planning — do I need to update wills, trusts, beneficiaries?
  • Education funding — are 529 plans on track?

🏥 Benefits & Insurance Transition

Understanding your options for health, life, and other benefits after leaving

⚠️ Important: Qualifying Life Event Losing employer coverage is a qualifying life event — this allows you (and dependents) to enroll in a new health plan outside of normal open enrollment windows. You typically have 30-60 days from your coverage end date.

Health Insurance Options

OptionProsConsKey Details
COBRA Same coverage, same doctors, immediate Very expensive (full premium + 2% admin fee), 18 months max Good as a bridge; you can switch to another plan later
Spouse's Employer Plan Often most affordable, ongoing coverage May need to change doctors/network Qualifying life event enables mid-year enrollment
ACA Marketplace Subsidies possible if income drops, many plan options Can be expensive without subsidies wahealthplanfinder.org (WA) or healthcare.gov
Private / Direct Choose any plan, no employer dependency Usually the most expensive option May require medical underwriting

Insurance Action Items

Get your COBRA cost estimate from the MS benefits portal
If you have a spouse who works — check if you/kids can be added to their plan
Check ACA marketplace options and potential subsidies for your income level
Verify your doctors are in the networks of potential new plans
Schedule all pending medical, dental, and vision appointments before coverage ends
Life insurance: get term life quotes NOW while still employed Easier to qualify and better rates while employed. Consider 20-year term for long-term coverage.
MS life insurance conversion — request paperwork within 31 days of losing group coverage
Review MS supplemental benefits: AD&D, legal plan, pet insurance, etc. Decide which to replace independently

HSA Strategy

💡 Your HSA is a Superpower Asset Your HSA balance is yours forever, tax-free for medical expenses at any age. After 65, it works like a traditional IRA for non-medical expenses. Keep it invested and growing. Save ALL medical receipts — you can reimburse yourself years or even decades later.

FSA Warning

⚠️ Use It or Lose It Unlike HSA, FSA funds typically must be used by your last day or within a short grace period. Plan to spend your FSA balance before departure — buy new glasses, stock up on eligible expenses.

🌟 Planning What's Next

Common paths and practical steps for life after Microsoft

First 30 Days — Decompress & Reset

Give yourself real time off — don't jump into the next thing immediately After years at MS, you've earned space to decompress. This is important for your mental health.
Establish a daily routine / structure (exercise, learning, family)
Complete all administrative transitions (insurance, accounts, rollovers)

Career Paths to Consider

💻 Consulting / Freelancing

  • Identify your consulting niche — what's your unique value from your MS experience?
  • Set up a professional online presence (website, LinkedIn optimization)
  • Reach out to your MS network for potential opportunities
  • Research market rates for your expertise — don't undersell yourself
  • Set up business infrastructure: LLC, invoicing, contracts, liability insurance
  • Consider platforms: Toptal, Expert360, GLG, or direct client relationships

🌱 Starting a Business / Startup

  • Define the problem you're solving and validate with potential customers
  • Set up business entity (LLC is usually best to start) — discuss S-Corp election with tax advisor
  • Set a budget cap — how much are you willing to invest before revenue?
  • If you have a co-founder, formalize roles and equity split early
  • Build an MVP before investing heavily
  • Review your MS non-compete — any restrictions on your planned business?

💼 Joining Another Company

  • Update your resume — focus on impact and outcomes, not just MS titles
  • Leverage your network — referrals are the #1 hiring channel
  • Consider company size/stage: big tech, mid-size, startup each offer different things
  • Negotiate hard on equity to compensate for forfeited MS RSUs
  • Check non-compete restrictions before interviewing with competitors

🌴 Full or Semi-Retirement

  • Work with your financial planner to confirm you can sustain your lifestyle
  • Consider part-time consulting or board work to stay engaged
  • Plan for healthcare until Medicare eligibility (65)
  • Explore volunteer work, teaching, mentoring, or passion projects

Business Setup Essentials

Register business entity (WA State LLC filing + EIN from IRS)
Open a business bank account (separate from personal)
Set up invoicing / accounting (QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Wave)
Get professional liability / E&O insurance if consulting
Discuss Solo 401(k) or SEP IRA for tax-advantaged retirement savings
Self-employed health insurance deduction — discuss with tax advisor

Personal Well-being

Build a health & fitness routine
Invest time in family and relationships
Build community connections outside of Microsoft Industry meetups, entrepreneur groups, local business associations, alumni networks
Learn new skills or get certifications that support your next chapter
